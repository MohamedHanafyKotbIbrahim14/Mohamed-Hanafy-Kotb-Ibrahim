About Me

I am a PhD candidate at UNSW Business School, working under the supervision of Dr. Eric C.K. Cheung, Dr. Andrés Villegas, and Dr. Jae-Kyung Woo.

My research focuses on the intersection of traditional actuarial science and modern machine learning techniques, with particular emphasis on telematics-based car insurance pricing. I develop novel frameworks that combine domain expertise with data-driven approaches to create more accurate and fair insurance pricing models.

Beyond research, I am passionate about teaching and have served as a Teaching Assistant for multiple courses in actuarial data science, risk analytics, and AI applications in insurance.

Current Projects

Ready for Submission

Credibility Premium Correction with CANN

A novel framework combining credibility theory with Combined Actuarial Neural Networks for improved premium estimation using telematics data. The approach integrates traditional actuarial domain knowledge with modern deep learning techniques.

CANN Telematics Credibility Theory Premium Pricing
In Progress

Driving Behavior Bonus-Malus Systems

Developing innovative Bonus-Malus Systems that incorporate telematics intensities—encompassing discrete, continuous, and percentage variables—moving beyond traditional discrete near-miss events.

Bonus-Malus Telematics Intensities Driving Behavior

Reinforcement Learning for Insurance Pricing

Comparing Q-learning, Value Iteration, and Linear Programming approaches for dynamic insurance pricing optimization. This research explores how RL can adapt premiums based on evolving risk profiles.
